# Shepherd AI — User Guide

**https://bs.thomasperdana.com**

Shepherd AI is an AI-powered KJV Bible study tool. Enter any passage, choose your study level, and get a complete study tailored to your audience — from 5th grade to PhD.

---

## Getting Started

### 1. Create an Account
1. Go to https://bs.thomasperdana.com
2. Click **Start Free**
3. Sign up with your email or Google account
4. You'll be taken to the study dashboard

### 2. Your First Study
1. Type a Bible passage in the search box (e.g., "John 3:16" or "Romans 8:28-30")
2. Choose your study level:
   - 🟢 **5th Grade** — Simple, concrete, for kids age 10-11
   - 🟡 **High School** — Real, relevant, for teens age 14-18
   - 🔵 **College** — Critical thinking, context, debate (default)
   - 🔴 **PhD** — Scholarly exegesis, original research
3. Click **Study →**
4. Your study appears with level-specific sections

### 3. What You Get (By Level)

**Level 1 — 5th Grader:**
- The Big Idea (one sentence)
- Memory Verse (with hand motion)
- Picture This (visual metaphor)
- Think About It (simple questions)
- Try This (hands-on activity)
- Talk to God (child's prayer)
- Show Someone (share with family)

**Level 2 — High Schooler:**
- The Hook (real teen scenario)
- The Text (verses with context)
- What It Really Means (plain English)
- The Clash (culture vs. Bible)
- Your Turn (reflection questions)
- This Week (concrete challenge)
- Prayer

**Level 3 — College:**
- Thesis Statement
- Text & Context (historical, literary, authorial)
- Word Study (Greek/Hebrew with Strong's numbers)
- Interpretive Options (scholarly views)
- Reformed View
- Theological Pitfalls
- Discussion Questions
- Research Assignment
- Teach It (outline for teaching others)

**Level 4 — PhD:**
- Research Question
- Exegetical Analysis (per-verse parsing)
- Philological Deep Dive (etymology, cognates, dictionaries)
- Scholarly Debate (named scholars, methodology critique)
- Reformed Position
- Pastoral Note
- Annotated Bibliography
- Systematic Theology Connection
- Sermon Prep (titles, outline, tough questions)
- Original Contribution
- 30-Day Research Plan

## Features

### ✝️ Christological Root
Every study begins with what Jesus said or did on the topic. This runs before every level — no exceptions.

### 📄 PDF Export
After any study, click **PDF** to download a print-ready copy for your group or class.

### 💡 Recommendation Engine
After completing a study, click **"What should I study next?"** to get 3 personalized recommendations:

- 🌱 **Beginner** — Foundation step. Safe, achievable, builds confidence.
- 🌿 **Intermediate** — Deeper exploration. Stretches understanding.
- 🌳 **Expert** — Scholarly path. Original contribution, teaching mastery.

Click a recommendation to journal it as your next action. Each recommendation:
- Names a specific Bible passage to study next
- Explains WHY it follows from what you just studied
- Gives a concrete action step

### 🔤 Strong's Concordance
Level 3 and 4 studies include original Greek/Hebrew word studies with Strong's numbers. Click any Strong's number for full definitions and usage.

### 📋 Study History
Your 20 most recent studies are saved. Click **History** to revisit any past study.

## Tips

- **Start at your comfort level.** Level 3 (College) is the default — it works for most adults.
- **Try different levels on the same passage.** See how John 3:16 changes from 5th grade to PhD.
- **Use the recommendation engine.** It learns from what you study and suggests logical next steps.
- **Export to PDF for groups.** Generate a study at your group's level, download the PDF, print and share.
- **Short passages work best.** Single verses or short ranges (2-5 verses) give the most focused results.
